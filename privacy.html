Privacy Policy

Last updated: 9 July 2026

The Cat Priestess is a small, independent project. Your privacy matters here — there are no accounts to create, and we never sell your information. This page explains, plainly, what is collected and why.

Analytics & cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand, in aggregate, how visitors find and use the site. Analytics cookies load only after you accept them in the cookie banner; if you decline, they are never set. Analytics data is anonymised where possible and is not used to identify you personally.

The Bastet chat

When you chat with Bastet, the messages you send are transmitted to a third-party AI provider (Google Gemini) so a reply can be generated. We do not store your conversations on our servers, and the chat resets when you leave. Please don't share sensitive personal, medical, legal, or financial details in the chat.

The contact form

If you send a message through the contact page, the name, email, and message you provide are delivered to us (via the form service FormSubmit) so we can reply. We use this only to respond to you.

On your device only

Your cookie choice, your reading preferences, and your Card-of-the-Day streak are saved in your browser's local storage — on your device, never sent to us.

Third-party services

  • Vercel — hosting.
  • Google Analytics — anonymous usage statistics (with consent).
  • Google Gemini — powers the Bastet chat replies.
  • FormSubmit — delivers contact-form messages.
  • Ko-fi — if you leave an offering; their own privacy terms apply.

Each of these has its own privacy policy governing the data it handles.

Your choices

You can decline analytics cookies at any time, choose not to use the chat or contact form, and clear your browser's local storage to remove anything stored on your device. To ask a question about your data, write to usivaylo@gmail.com.

The readings and Bastet's words are offered for reflection, insight, and entertainment. They are not a substitute for professional medical, legal, financial, or psychological advice. Always trust your own judgement and seek a qualified professional when you need one.
